# Event Report: “AWS Builders Meetup”

### Purpose of the Event

- Share effective AWS learning and hands-on practice experiences.
- Introduce tools that support learning, product development, and Hackathon participation.
- Inspire participants to develop soft skills alongside technical expertise.
- Help students build a learning roadmap and prepare for careers in the technology industry.

### Speakers

- **Huỳnh Thái Linh** – *Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci*
- **The Ballers Team** (Huỳnh An Khương, Mai Quốc Anh, Nguyễn Trần Minh Quân) – *Hackathon: More Like "HA! A Tons of Fun"*
- **Nguyễn Thị Quỳnh Như** – *Why We Always Need Confidence*
- **Phạm Khắc Uy** – *The Iceberg of Procrastination*

### Key Highlights

#### Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci

The speaker shared common challenges faced by beginners when learning AWS, such as unexpected cloud costs, forgetting to delete resources, or hesitating to practice on a real AWS environment. To address these issues, the speaker introduced **AWS Cloud Quest**, a gamified learning platform that allows learners to gain practical AWS experience by completing interactive challenges.

In addition, **Floci** was introduced as an open-source tool that simulates AWS services on a local machine, enabling developers to test applications without deploying them to the cloud. A recommended learning roadmap was presented: **Cloud Quest → Floci → Real AWS**, helping learners gradually improve their skills while minimizing cloud expenses.

#### Hackathon – More Like "HA! A Tons of Fun"

The **Ballers Team** shared their experience of participating in hackathons, where participants must brainstorm ideas, develop products, and present their solutions within a limited timeframe.

The speakers introduced several projects, including **UTMorpho**, an AI-powered UI generation tool with direct canvas editing, **SynthHunter**, an AI-based voice detection system that distinguishes human voices from AI-generated voices, and **Vortex**, a recruitment support platform. They also shared valuable lessons about teamwork, time management, and problem-solving under pressure.

#### Why We Always Need Confidence

This session emphasized that confidence does not mean being the smartest or the most perfect person. Instead, it is about having the courage to take the first step and being willing to learn despite uncertainty.

The speaker introduced two well-known concepts: **Imposter Syndrome** and the **Dunning-Kruger Effect**, while also sharing practical techniques for building confidence, such as the **5-Second Rule**, celebrating small achievements, and continuously improving technical skills.

#### The Iceberg of Procrastination

The speaker explained that procrastination is not simply caused by laziness. In most cases, it is driven by hidden fears, such as fear of failure, fear of being judged, or feeling inadequate.

To overcome procrastination, the speaker introduced the **Name Your Fear** technique, which helps identify the root cause of avoidance, along with the **5-Minute Rule**, encouraging people to start working for just five minutes to build momentum.

### Event Experience

The **AWS Builders Meetup** provided valuable insights not only into AWS technologies but also into personal development. Each presentation was practical, engaging, and supported by real-world experiences from the speakers, making it easy for participants to understand and apply the knowledge.

The session on **Cloud Quest** and **Floci** introduced useful tools for practicing AWS without worrying about cloud costs. Meanwhile, the stories shared by **The Ballers Team** inspired me to confidently participate in Hackathons and develop real-world technology projects.

Although the final two presentations focused on confidence and overcoming procrastination rather than technical topics, they were highly relevant to students. Practical techniques such as the **5-Second Rule** and the **5-Minute Rule** are simple strategies that can be applied immediately in both academic and professional settings.
